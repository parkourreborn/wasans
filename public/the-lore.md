# **The Lore Book of Wasans**

## **Chapter 1: The Rise of the Wasans**

**➤ The Beginning**
The Wasans began with a single voice — a runner who sought truth and redemption. They weren’t the fastest or the strongest, but they saw what others couldn’t: that creating routes is a skill, a form of art that takes courage, vision, and relentless effort.

The First Wasan, known only as “Wasan”, spoke openly about their journey:

“I am Wasan. I’m trying to explain my mistakes and my path. I didn’t get a boost — I admit that I once did, but I worked to master my own routes. Making a route is a skill. It takes vision to see a gap and skill to cross it. Those who mock me for being a ‘router’ don’t understand the effort it takes to create a new path.”

This statement sparked a movement. Runners who felt unheard or misunderstood began to gather, sharing routes and defending each other against accusations and judgment. Wasan believed that speed alone wasn’t enough — creativity and resilience mattered just as much.

**➤ The Fall of the Old Ranks**
The old ranking system was broken. Corrupted by data loss, accusations of rank trading, and unfair bans, the Wasans rejected it entirely. They believed that skill couldn’t be measured by a leaderboard — it had to be proven in motion.

Some names, like “chr” and “headrog”, became symbols of those who endured wrongful accusations. They lost their ranks, but their skill remained undeniable. The Wasans embraced them as heroes who refused to give up.

**➤ The Alt Accords**
The Wasans also acknowledged the reality of alt accounts. Many runners, banned permanently, still returned to the city on alternate accounts — not to cheat, but because they loved the game too much to stay away. Wasan argued that these players weren’t criminals — they were survivors of an unfair system.

“They use alts because they still want to run. They don’t want to break the game — they just want to play it.”
